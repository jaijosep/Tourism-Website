Places to Visit in Kottayam

Kumarakom

The village of Kumarakom is a cluster of little islands on the Vembanad Lake, and is part of the Kuttanad region. The bird sanctuary here, which is spread across 14 acres is a favourite haunt of migratory birds and an ornithologist's paradise. Egrets, Darters, Herons, Teals, Waterfowls, Cuckoo, Wild Duck and migratory birds like the Siberian Stork visit here in flocks and fascinate all visitors. An enchanting backwater destination, Kumarakom offers visitors many other leisure options. Boating and fishing facilities are available at the Taj Garden Retreat, a sprawling old bungalow-turned-resort. Houseboats and traditional Kettuvalloms (rice barges) offer great experiences.

Illickal Kallu

One of the most popular tourist destinations in Kottayam district is Illickal Kallu, situated on a majestic hill called Illickal Mala. There are numerous mountain streams in Illickal Mala, which is 6,000 ft above sea level, and they flow down to form the serene Meenachil River. Each of the three hills has a peculiar shape. One of them resembles a mushroom and hence, its name ‘Kuda Kallu’ (umbrella-shaped rock). The second hill has a small hunch on the side and is therefore called as ‘Koonu Kallu’ (hunchback rock). The third one is called Illickal Kallu as it derives its name from the mountain itself. The steep rock on the top of the hill has a part of its peak shorn off. The breathtaking aerial view of the surroundings is sure to overwhelm you.

Vagamon

A popular tourist destination in Kerala renowned for its velvet lawns and towering pine tree forests is Vagamon. The lush green meadows, serene valleys and grassy hills make it evenmore beautiful. Visitors can avail many activities including trekking, paragliding, mountaineering and rock climbing. Vagamon located on the Idukki-Kottayam border is also rich with wide varieties of flora and fauna. Vagamon is also known for its pine forest which offers visitors a panoramoic spot for phtography, and for unwinding themselves taking strolls through the rows of pine trees.
